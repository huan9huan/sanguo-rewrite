# Prelude to Chaos

An old song says the great river rolls east, washing heroes downstream like foam. Success and ruin — gone in a glance. The green hills remain. A few old men drink cheap wine on the bank and laugh about everything that ever happened.

---

The empire splits. It always has. Sooner or later, someone puts it back together — until it splits again.

The Han had lasted four hundred years. But by the time Emperors Huan and Ling sat on the throne, the dynasty was breathing hard.

The rot started with the eunuchs.

Emperor Huan refused to employ good men. He trusted only the eunuchs around him. When Emperor Ling took the throne, the general Dou Wu and the grand tutor Chen Fan tried to wipe out the eunuch faction together. The plot leaked. Both men were killed instead. After that, the only people who mattered in court served behind the walls.

In the second year of Jian Ning, the fourth month, Emperor Ling had just taken his seat in the Wende Hall. A strange wind swept through the corners of the chamber.

A great green snake dropped from the rafters and coiled itself on the dragon throne.

The emperor collapsed. His attendants dragged him away. The officials scattered. The snake vanished soon after — but the omens did not stop. Earthquakes. Seawater flooding the fields. Black vapor pouring straight into the Wende Hall. The portents grew worse year after year.

A court official named Cai Yong submitted a memorial blaming the disasters on eunuch interference in state affairs. Emperor Ling read it, sighed, and rose to change his clothes.

Behind him, the eunuch Cao Jie watched.

Soon after, Cai Yong was found guilty of some invented charge and sent back to his hometown.

Ten eunuchs bound themselves into a faction — Zhang Rang, Zhao Zhong, Duan Gui, Cao Jie, Jian Shuo, and others. People called them the Ten Attendants. Emperor Ling addressed Zhang Rang as "Father." By then the people's faith in the court was gone, and bandits roamed everywhere.

---

In Julu Commandery lived three brothers. The eldest was Zhang Jiao.

Zhang Jiao was a failed scholar who never passed the imperial exams. He went into the mountains to gather herbs and encountered an old man with green eyes and a white beard. The old man handed him three scrolls.

"This is the Way of Great Peace. Use it to save people. Do not use it for evil."

Then he dissolved into wind and was gone.

Zhang Jiao studied the scrolls and learned to heal with talisman water. In the first year of Zhongping, plague spread across the land. He traveled from village to village, healing the sick, asking nothing in return. His followers multiplied. He organized them into thirty-six districts across eight provinces.

On every door, two characters appeared: *Jiazi.*

The slogan was one line:

*The Blue Heaven is dead. The Yellow Heaven rises. The year is Jiazi. All under heaven will prosper.*

Zhang Jiao secretly forged yellow banners and sent a man to contact the eunuch Feng Xu inside the palace — an inside ally, ready to strike when the day came. The plan was thorough. But one of his own disciples, Tang Zhou, walked straight into a government office and betrayed everything.

The court seized Ma Yuanyi and executed him. Feng Xu was thrown into prison.

Zhang Jiao did not wait. That night he raised his army. He named himself General of Heaven. His second brother Zhang Bao became General of Earth, and the youngest, Zhang Liang, General of Man. He shouted to his followers: "The Han's mandate is ending. Those who follow Heaven will live!"

Four or five hundred thousand people wrapped yellow cloth around their heads — the Yellow Turbans — and rose with him. Government troops fled at the sight.

---

Word reached Luoyang. The general-in-chief He Jin petitioned for an imperial decree: each region would defend itself and raise troops locally. At the same time, three columns of regular forces marched out — under Lu Zhi, Huangfu Song, and Zhu Jun, each leading elite soldiers, striking in different directions.

Zhang Jiao's first army bore down on Youzhou.

The governor of Youzhou was Liu Yan, a man of the imperial Liu clan. When word of the approaching rebels reached him, he summoned his field officer Zou Jing.

Zou Jing said, "They outnumber us, Governor. We need to recruit. Now."

Liu Yan posted the notice that same day.

Within days, the same sheet of paper appeared on every city gate across Youzhou's counties.

No one knew yet who it would bring.
