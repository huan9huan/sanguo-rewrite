# The Notice at the Gate

NARRATOR: Empires rarely break all at once.

NARRATOR: At first, the sound is small.

NARRATOR: A strange wind moves through a palace hall.

LISTENER: This is where Three Kingdoms begins?

NARRATOR: Not with armies. Not yet.

NARRATOR: It begins with Emperor Ling taking his seat.

NARRATOR: Then a great green snake drops from the rafters.

NARRATOR: It coils on the dragon throne.

LISTENER: The dragon throne means the emperor's seat, right?

NARRATOR: Yes. The seat of the emperor, and the symbol of the empire.

LISTENER: So the symbol of power becomes a warning.

NARRATOR: Exactly. And the warnings keep coming.

NARRATOR: Earthquakes. Flooded fields. Black vapor rising into the hall.

NARRATOR: A court official named Cai Yong says the rot starts with the eunuchs.

LISTENER: Eunuchs were palace officials close to the emperor?

NARRATOR: Yes. They controlled doors, messages, and sometimes the emperor himself.

LISTENER: Cai Yong tells the truth. What happens to him?

NARRATOR: He is sent home on an invented charge.

NARRATOR: When a court stops listening, hungry people listen somewhere else.

NARRATOR: Far from the capital, a failed scholar named Zhang Jiao gathers herbs.

NARRATOR: In the mountains, an old man gives him three scrolls.

LISTENER: So he is not a general yet.

NARRATOR: No. First, he is a healer.

NARRATOR: Plague spreads. Zhang Jiao moves from village to village.

NARRATOR: He heals the sick and asks for nothing.

NARRATOR: His followers multiply across eight provinces.

LISTENER: That is no longer charity. That is power.

NARRATOR: Then a slogan begins to move through the land.

NARRATOR: The Blue Heaven is dead.

NARRATOR: The Yellow Heaven rises.

LISTENER: Blue Heaven, Yellow Heaven. Is that a call for a new order?

NARRATOR: Yes. The old world is dead. A new one should replace it.

NARRATOR: The plan is careful. Then a disciple betrays it.

LISTENER: So Zhang Jiao has to move first.

NARRATOR: That night, he raises his army.

NARRATOR: Hundreds of thousands wrap yellow cloth around their heads.

NARRATOR: The Yellow Turbans rise. Government troops run.

NARRATOR: The news reaches Luoyang, the imperial capital, and the court finally panics.

NARRATOR: Every region is told to raise troops.

NARRATOR: In Youzhou, a northern region, Governor Liu Yan posts a recruitment notice.

LISTENER: One notice on a city gate. Why does it matter?

NARRATOR: Because it will bring a poor man to history's door.
